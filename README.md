## Outline
To achieve climate goals, states like New York are accelerating their transition from fossil fuels to renewable energy sources. Concurrently,
the escalating frequency of extreme weather events due to climate change poses significant challenges to energy systems. Given the in-
terconnectedness of energy systems with socio-economic dynamics such as poverty, the resilience of energy infrastructures is paramount
to mitigate adverse societal impacts. While there exists foundational research in this domain, the exploration of climate change impacts
on energy systems remains relatively underexplored. The existing literature comprises a limited number of comprehensive studies ad-
dressing this critical intersection in contrast to sectors like agriculture and water management, which have seen extensive research into
climate change impacts.
